The Milky Way Galaxy

Our galaxy is called the Milky Way. It is a barred spiral galaxy that includes our Solar System. The Milky Way is about 100,000 light-years across and contains between 100 and 400 billion stars. Our solar system is located about 30,000 light-years from the galactic center. Learn more about the Milky Way.

"The Solar System"
The Solar System is situated in a small arm of the Milky Way called the Orion Arm, which lies between the larger Sagittarius and Perseus arms. It is approximately 27,000 light-years from the Galactic Center. The Solar System orbits the center of the Milky Way at a speed of about 515,000 mph, taking roughly 230 million years to complete one full orbit.
